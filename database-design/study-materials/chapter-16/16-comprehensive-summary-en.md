*Database Design for Mere Mortals®, 25th Anniversary Edition — Fourth Edition*

## Overview

Chapter 16 is the **closing chapter** of the book—a reflective conclusion that affirms your accomplishments and encourages continued learning. No new technical content; it reinforces the value of the design method and offers parting advice for your journey ahead.

---

## Key Messages

### You Have What You Need

- Designing a database properly **doesn't require being a rocket scientist**
- It's a **relatively straightforward task** for anyone with good common sense
- Following a good design method → sound and reliable database structure
- You now possess the **knowledge and skills** to design a relational database
- You know how to define structures, establish relationships, implement data integrity
- You know how to **improve** improperly or poorly designed structures

### Learning Is Ongoing

- Database design is an **ever-continuing process**
- Options: Learn enough for your needs, turn it into a profession, or make it a lifelong study
- **The more you learn, the more you realize you don't know it all**—true of any major subject (music, acting, art, philosophy)
- Don't be discouraged

### Parting Advice: Seven Words

**Always learn. Take risks. Never say never.**

- **Learn** — Opens doors to new things, fresh ideas, different concepts
- **Take risks** — Great way to change course; "Nothing ventured, nothing gained"
- **Never say never** — The Universe has a funny way of saying "Not so much..."

### The Journey

- **A journey begins with but one step**
- You've taken the first step by reading this book
- Now continue by learning about other facets of database management
- **The book ends here, but your journey is just beginning.**
